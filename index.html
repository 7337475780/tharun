INDIAN MYTHOLOGY

Know about the indian history and its greatness.

Indian mythology is a vast and vibrant tapestry of stories, spanning thousands of years and encompassing a rich variety of characters, from benevolent deities to fearsome demons, mythical creatures, and epic heroes. Here's a glimpse into this fascinating world:

Source of the Stories: Hinduism, the world's oldest religion, is the foundation of Indian mythology. Stories are found in ancient texts like the Vedas, epics like the Mahabharata and Ramayana, and the Puranas.

Key Concepts: Devas and Asuras: The cosmic stage is filled with gods (devas) and demons (asuras) who are locked in an eternal struggle between good and evil. Popular devas include Vishnu, Shiva, Brahma, Ganesha, and Durga.

Dharma and Karma: Dharma refers to righteous duty and moral law, while karma is the law of cause and effect. These concepts are central to understanding the stories and their lessons.

Reincarnation and Moksha: The belief in rebirth and the ultimate goal of achieving moksha, liberation from the cycle of rebirth.

Famous Epics: Mahabharata: An epic poem considered the longest ever written, it tells the story of a dynastic war between cousins, filled with tales of duty, love, and loss.

Ramayana: Another epic poem, it narrates the story of Rama, the ideal prince, his wife Sita, and their battle against the demon king Ravana.

Beyond Hinduism: Indian mythology extends beyond Hinduism, with stories and legends from other religions like Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism also contributing to the rich tapestry.
